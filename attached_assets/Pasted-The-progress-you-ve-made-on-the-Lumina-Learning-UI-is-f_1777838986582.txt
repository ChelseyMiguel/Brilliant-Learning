## 1. The "Amber State" Logic for Marbles
In your screenshot, the question asks for the probability of a red marble. A common mistake (the **Amber State**) is for students to provide the **Ratio** ($3:7$) instead of the **Probability** ($3/10$).

> **Prompt:** "I am building an Interaction Card for a 'Marbles in a Bag' probability question. 
> * **Correct Answer:** $3/10$ (Trigger Emerald success state).
> * **Common Misconception (Amber):** If the user selects $3/7$, trigger the Amber state. 
> * **Amber Hint:** 'You’ve compared red marbles to blue marbles (a ratio). Remember, probability is the number of successful outcomes divided by the **total** number of marbles in the bag.'
> * **Distractor (Red):** If the user selects $7/10$, provide a hint: 'That is the probability of picking a **blue** marble. Check the question again!'"

---

## 2. The "Marble Sandbox" Learning Center
Before the user sees the card in your screenshot, let's give them a **Learning Center** where they can build the bag themselves.

> **Prompt:** "Create a 'Bag Builder' Learning Center for Lumina Learning.
> * **Interface:** A `slate-50` workspace with a central 'Bag' SVG and two buttons: '+ Red' and '+ Blue.'
> * **Interaction:** As the user adds marbles, they physically drop into the bag with `framer-motion` physics.
> * **Live Analytics:** Below the bag, show a real-time 'Probability Meter' that updates a fraction as the total count changes.
> * **Discovery Goal:** Ask the user to: 'Add enough marbles so that your chance of picking a Red is exactly 50%.' 
> * **Bridge:** Once they hit the goal, the 'Start Practice' button appears, leading to the question in my screenshot."

---

## 3. Visual Polish: The "Ghost Marble" Feedback
To make the "Check Answer" moment more satisfying, we can animate the result.

> **Prompt:** "Design a 'Feedback Animation' for the marble question.
> * **Action:** When the user clicks 'Check Answer,' an SVG of the 'Bag' appears. 
> * **Success:** 3 Red marbles glow brightly in `emerald-500` while the others fade out.
> * **Failure:** If they pick $3/7$, highlight all 7 blue marbles in `amber-400` to visually show that they forgot to include them in the total count.
> * **LaTeX Integration:** Simultaneously render the equation: $P(\text{Red}) = \frac{3}{3+7} = \frac{3}{10}$."
